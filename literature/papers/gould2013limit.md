abstract: We survey the properties of limit order books (LOBs). We begin by describing a LOB, explaining how orders are submitted, modified, and matched. We then discuss the key empirical regularities and stylized facts of LOB data, and review theoretical models from both economics and physics perspectives. The economics models focus on strategic behavior of agents, while physics models focus on statistical properties of order flow. We conclude by identifying important open questions in the field.
